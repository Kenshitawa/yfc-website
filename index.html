Who We Are

Youth for Children (YFC)

Founded in 2023 by Stephen Okoth and Joy Tadayo, YFC began as a capstone project addressing inequalities in education. Through close interactions with learners, they discovered widespread abuse and created a Child Protection and Safeguarding curriculum (CPAS) that reached 88 children in its first phase.

Today YFC operates in Kisumu, Machakos, Kilifi, and Nairobi, running youth‑led programs that protect children, promote inclusive education, and raise a new generation of ethical leaders.

Our Vision

A just and thriving society where every child is protected, educated, and empowered by the leadership of inspired youth.

Our Mission

Through youth‑led programs, we strengthen child protection, inclusive education, and community development – building a future where young people lead efforts to safeguard and empower the next generation.

Our Impact

255 children reached in 2025 across 5 schools in 4 counties, 18 youth leaders trained, Children Rights Clubs established, and programs expanding to physical sessions nationwide.

Our Work

Core Programs

Youth‑led initiatives that build upon children's capacity to respond to the contemporary world through digital storytelling, safeguarding, and community action.

Child Protection & Safeguarding

Creating safe environments where children learn and thrive free from abuse, neglect, and violence through awareness, workshops, and community engagement.

Empowering the Future

Flagship youth leadership program strengthening young voices through mentorship, public speaking, storytelling, and hands‑on community engagement.

Climate Action (Eco‑Heroes)

Equipping children with knowledge and skills for environmental stewardship through storytelling, tree‑growing, waste management, and conservation activities.

One Learner, One Pen

Resource mobilization initiative providing pens, pencils, books, and school supplies to children from underserved and informal settlement communities.

Inclusive Education

Creating learning environments where every child feels safe, valued, and empowered to reach their full potential through rights‑based approaches.

Children Rights Clubs

Establishing platforms in schools across Kenya where children can voice their concerns and advocate for their rights among peers and communities.
